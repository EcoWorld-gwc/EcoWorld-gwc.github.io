Endangered Animals

There is 50,000 of species in the rainforest of south america. Though, there are millions of different animals residing in South America, however, there is no definite figure about the exact number of animal population. South America is home to roughly 40% of the world’s plant and animal species, including the Amazonian Manatee, Black Rhinoceros, the Ethiopian Wolf, and the Grevy's Zebra.

Amazonian Manatee

With a population set to decline by 30% over the next three generations, in part due to continued hunting pressure, Amazonian manatees are now threatened by habitat loss and mercury pollution from illegal goldmining.

Golden Lion Tamarin

Found only in the Atlantic forest of Brazil, these tiny monkeys are under threat from agriculture-driven deforestation, which continues to fragment their forest home, severing links between populations.

Galapagos Giant Tortoise

Galapagos tortoise populations were historically decimated by sailors who stored them on ships to use as a source of food and water on long Pacific voyages. In the modern day, the tortoises are being edged out of their habitats by non-native goats that were introduced by settlers- goats decimate local plant life, leaving the tortoises with no food.

Waved Albatross

The Waved Albatross is threatened in part because of habitat loss on the only island on which it breeds (Española Island), due to overgrazing caused by non-native goats. However, the biggest threats to albatrosses are climate change and overfishing, which have driven the fish stocks on which these birds depend to near extinction, as well as the fact that albatrosses are frequently caught as bycatch of longline fishing.
